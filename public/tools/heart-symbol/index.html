How Heart Symbols Work

Heart symbols are Unicode characters, which means they are standardized text characters recognized by virtually every modern device and platform. Unlike images or custom fonts, Unicode hearts are pure text. When you copy a heart symbol from this page and paste it into Instagram, Twitter, Facebook, Discord, WhatsApp, or any other app, it will display correctly without needing any special software. The classic heart ♥ (U+2665) has been part of Unicode since version 1.1 in 1993, making it one of the most universally supported symbols in computing.

Types of Heart Symbols

Classic Hearts include the fundamental heart characters from the Unicode standard. The black heart suit ♥ and white heart suit ♡ come from the playing card symbols block. The heavy black heart ❤ is from the Dingbats block and serves as the base character for the red heart emoji ❤️.

Colored Hearts are emoji hearts that display in specific colors on supported platforms. These include 💙 Blue Heart, 💚 Green Heart, 💛 Yellow Heart, 🧡 Orange Heart, 💜 Purple Heart, 🖤 Black Heart, 🤍 White Heart, and 🤎 Brown Heart. Each colored heart emoji carries its own cultural meaning in online communication.

Decorative Hearts feature animated-style emoji like 💗 Growing Heart, 💓 Beating Heart, 💖 Sparkling Heart, 💕 Two Hearts, and 💞 Revolving Hearts. These add dynamic visual flair to your messages and posts.
