How to Count Words in Your Text

Counting words is essential for meeting requirements in essays, blog posts, articles, cover letters, and social media. Here's how our word counter works:

  1. Paste or type your text into the box above. The counter updates in real time as you type.
  2. Read the stats — words, characters (with and without spaces), sentences, paragraphs, and more appear instantly.
  3. Check reading time — based on average reading speed of 238 words per minute (Brysbaert, 2019) and speaking speed of 150 words per minute.

All counting happens in your browser. Your text is never sent to any server.

What to Know Before You Copy and Paste

This tool page is designed to answer the practical questions people usually have after landing on a word counter result: what to copy, where it works, what can break, and which related text style should be used instead.

Best Use Case

Use this page for practical text cleanup, counting, repetition, invisible text, or conversion tasks. The goal is not just to make text look different, but to help you choose a style that fits the field, platform, audience, and copy-paste workflow.

What To Check Before You Paste

  • Compatibility: preview the output on the platform where you will use it, especially on mobile.
  • Readability: use decorative Unicode for short emphasis, names, labels, and separators rather than long paragraphs.
  • Searchability: keep hashtags, handles, important keywords, and contact information in plain text when discovery matters.
  • Accessibility: screen readers and older devices may announce or display styled Unicode differently from normal text.
