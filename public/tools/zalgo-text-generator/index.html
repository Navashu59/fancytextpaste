Zalgo Text Generator - Create Creepy Glitch Text

The Zalgo text generator transforms ordinary text into chaotic, distorted, "corrupted" looking text by stacking dozens of Unicode combining diacritical marks above, through, and below each character. Named after the internet creepypasta entity "Zalgo," this glitch text effect has become a staple of internet culture, Discord servers, and creative social media posts.

Three Intensity Levels

Our generator offers three levels of Zalgo distortion. Light adds subtle combining marks for a slightly unsettling appearance that remains readable. Medium applies moderate stacking for a clearly glitched effect. Insane pushes the combining characters to their extreme, creating a wall of overlapping marks that engulfs the original text in chaos.

How Zalgo Text Works

Every character in Unicode can have combining diacritical marks attached to it. These marks were designed for accents and linguistic notation, but when stacked in large numbers they produce the signature Zalgo distortion. The generator randomly selects from over 100 combining characters in three categories: marks that appear above the text, marks that appear through the middle, and marks that extend below the baseline. Unicode Reference & Technical Details

Zalgo text is made possible by the Unicode Standard's combining diacritical marks (U+0300–U+036F) and additional combining characters from other blocks.

Key Combining Character Ranges

  • Combining Diacritical Marks (U+0300–U+036F): The primary block for accents, cedillas, macrons, and other marks that combine with base characters.
  • Combining Diacritical Marks Extended (U+1AB0–U+1AFF): Additional historic and linguistic marks used in phonetic transcription.
  • Combining Diacritical Marks Supplement (U+1DC0–U+1DFF): Marks for medieval studies, Uralic phonetics, and more.
  • Combining Diacritical Marks for Symbols (U+20D0–U+20FF): Marks used with mathematical symbols and currency signs.

Each combining character is assigned a combining class that determines its stacking order and position relative to the base character. Zalgo generators exploit these properties by attaching multiple marks from different classes, causing them to render above, through, and below the text.

For authoritative reference, consult the official Unicode Character Charts and UTN #44: Unicode Character Database.
