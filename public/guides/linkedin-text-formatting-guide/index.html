LinkedIn text formatting guide

LinkedIn still does not give normal feed posts a bold or italic toolbar. If you want posts and profile sections that are easier to scan, the practical path is Unicode formatting used sparingly and in the right places. This guide shows what works, what to avoid, and which fields benefit most.

By Alex Chen|Updated May 29, 2026
Quick answer: Use the LinkedIn font generator for bold and italic text in headlines, post hooks, and short emphasis lines. For the cleanest look, keep most of the post in regular text and use formatting only on headers, key numbers, and calls to attention.

How to Bold Text on LinkedIn

LinkedIn posts don't support native bold formatting, but you can use Unicode bold characters that look bold on every device. Here's how:

  1. Go to FancyTextPaste LinkedIn Font Generator
  2. Type or paste your text in the input box
  3. Select the Bold or Bold Sans style
  4. Click the copy button
  5. Paste into your LinkedIn post

Example:

Regular: "5 tips for better networking"
Bold Unicode: "𝗗𝗲𝗳𝗳𝗲𝗿 𝗻𝗲𝘀𝘄𝗼𝗿𝗸𝗶𝗻𝗴 𝘀𝗶𝗽𝘀"

Unicode bold works because these are actual characters in the Unicode standard (Mathematical Bold letters), not formatting markup. They display correctly on every device and browser.

The safest formatting pattern for LinkedIn posts

  1. Use one bold hook line to stop the scroll.
  2. Use plain text for the story or explanation so readability stays high.
  3. Use bullets or short bold headers when you need to break up a list.
  4. End with a plain-language question instead of stacking more formatting at the bottom.

How to Italicize Text on LinkedIn

  2. Type your text
  3. Select the Italic or Italic Sans style
  4. Copy and paste into LinkedIn

Italic text is perfect for quotes, emphasis, or book/article titles in your LinkedIn posts.

LinkedIn Post Formatting Best Practices

Well-formatted posts get more engagement. Here are the formatting rules top LinkedIn creators follow:

  1. Start with a bold hook — Your first line determines if people click "see more." Make it bold using Unicode.
  2. Use single-sentence paragraphs — Short paragraphs are easier to read on mobile (where 60%+ of LinkedIn users are).
  3. Add white space — Leave a blank line between each point. Walls of text get scrolled past.
  4. Use bullet points for lists of 3+ — Lists are scannable. Paragraphs are not.
  5. Bold key numbers and insights — If you share a stat like "47% increase," make it bold so it pops.
  6. End with a CTA or question — Posts that ask a question get 50%+ more comments.

LinkedIn Profile Formatting Tips

You can use Unicode formatting in several parts of your LinkedIn profile:

  • Headline: Add separators like | or • between specialties. Example: "Marketing Director | B2B SaaS | Growth Strategy"
  • About section: Use Unicode bold for section headers and bullet points for key achievements
  • Experience descriptions: Use bullet points and bold for metrics (e.g., "Grew revenue 47% in 12 months")
  • Featured section: Bold titles in your featured posts and articles

What usually hurts readability

  • Using decorative fonts that make a professional profile look spammy.
  • Turning every sentence into bold text so nothing stands out anymore.
  • Using special characters that look broken or hard to read on mobile.
  • Stuffing too many symbols into the headline instead of keeping the meaning clear.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I bold text on LinkedIn?

LinkedIn doesn't have a native bold button for posts. To create bold text, use a Unicode bold text generator. Type your text, select the bold style, copy it, and paste it into your LinkedIn post. The bold characters are Unicode symbols (Mathematical Bold alphabet) that display as bold on any device without any special formatting support from LinkedIn.

Can I use italic text on LinkedIn?

Yes, you can use italic text on LinkedIn using Unicode italic characters. LinkedIn doesn't have a built-in italic button for posts, but you can generate italic text using a font generator tool, then copy and paste it. The italic characters are part of the Unicode standard and display correctly on all devices and browsers.

Does LinkedIn support bullet points?

LinkedIn articles support bullet points natively through the rich text editor. However, LinkedIn posts (regular feed posts) do not have a bullet button. You can add bullet points manually by typing or pasting bullet characters like •, ▪, ▸, or ✓ at the start of each line. Many people also use emojis as visual bullet points for a more casual tone.

Will formatted text hurt my LinkedIn engagement?

No, formatted text generally increases LinkedIn engagement. Posts with bold headers, bullet points, and visual structure get more reads because they're easier to scan. LinkedIn's algorithm favors posts with higher dwell time (time spent reading), and well-formatted posts keep people reading longer. However, don't overdo it — use formatting to highlight key points, not to make every word bold or italic.

Does Unicode bold text affect LinkedIn SEO?

Unicode bold text doesn't negatively affect LinkedIn's internal search indexing. LinkedIn indexes the content of your posts regardless of formatting. However, keep in mind that Unicode characters may not be searchable by exact keyword match on some platforms, so use regular text for important keywords and save bold/italic formatting for emphasis and headers within your post.
